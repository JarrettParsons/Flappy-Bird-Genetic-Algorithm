Here are some things to note:
The score in the top left represents the maximum tubes that have been crossed in one generation
The number in the top right represents what generation the currently displayed "birds" are from
The slider directly above controls the speed of the program (I would not recomend high speeds until only a few birds remain)
There are 200 birds per generation and on average it take 100 generations to produce a perfect bird (a perfect bird is one that will never die)
If there were more birds per generation then it would achieve a perfect bird in less generations but it would slow the program down considerably
